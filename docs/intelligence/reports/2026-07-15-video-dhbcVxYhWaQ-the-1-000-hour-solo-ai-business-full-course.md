### Full Analysis

Corey Gannon's AI Tools Assessment is a fully productized consulting model targeting small businesses (2–20 employees, $500K–$5M revenue) that are aware of AI but stuck at ChatGPT. The core offer is a $999 structured audit: a 45-minute discovery call, AI-assisted transcript analysis via Claude, a templated report built in Claude Design, and a 30-minute review call walking the client through tool prescriptions. The guarantee — find at least 5 hours per week saved or 100% refunded — removes all perceived risk for the client while forcing the consultant to deliver a minimum viable outcome every time.

Claude sits at the centre of the delivery stack. In Phase 2 the transcript is fed directly to Claude, which searches for off-the-shelf tools matching each pain point. Quality assurance is a human step — the consultant checks for category mismatches such as Salesforce prescribed to a four-person landscaping firm — but after three or four engagements the Claude skill is dialled in enough to produce copy-paste-ready recommendations. This is a direct proof-of-concept for AIOS: a skill that improves its own output quality through accumulated examples and structured context, exactly the pattern AIOS should systematise.

The upstream AI Concierge retainer is where the model reaches its highest leverage. Two 45-minute calls per month at $1,200–$2,000 yields an effective hourly rate above $1,000. Sessions follow the AOA loop (Audit → Optimize → Automate) applied to real client workflows; the deliverable is finished Claude skills built collaboratively on-screen. A Notion engagement hub tracks every completed skill, making value visible and renewals frictionless. Unlimited Voxer access is included but generates almost no actual messages — very high perceived value, near-zero delivery cost.

Three AIOS patterns are worth extracting and systematising. First, the effort-versus-impact matrix (quick wins vs. major projects) is a reusable triage framework applicable to any AI implementation engagement. Second, the onboarding sequence — connect tools, create context files, write global instructions, demonstrate a scheduled task and a skill — maps directly to a canonical AIOS client onboarding design. Third, the AOA framework itself (Audit, Optimize, Automate) is a three-step loop that can be baked into a reusable Claude skill and applied to any workflow a client describes, making it a strong candidate for a standard AIOS consulting pattern.
